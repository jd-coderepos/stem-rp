Using data collected by the FOCUS experiment at Fermilab, we report the discovery of the decay modes D 0→K − π + π + π + π − π − and D 0→π + π + π + π − π − π −. With a sample of 48±10 reconstructed D 0→K − π + π + π + π − π − decays and 149±17 reconstructed D 0→π + π + π + π − π − π − decays, we measure the following relative branching ratios: Γ(D0→K−π+π+π+π−π− )/Γ(D0→K−π+π+π− )=(2.70±0.58±0.38)×10−3, Γ(D0→π+π+π+π−π−π− )/Γ(D0→K−π+π+π− )=(5.23±0.59±1.35)×10−3, Γ(D0→π+π+π+π−π−π− )/Γ(D0→K−π+π+π+π−π− )=1.93±0.47±0.48. The first errors are statistical and the second are systematic. The branching fraction of the Cabibbo suppressed six-body decay mode is measured to be a factor of two higher than the branching fraction of the Cabibbo favored six-body decay mode.
