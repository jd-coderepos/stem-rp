Seven species of Stereum Hill:Pers. are recognised for South Africa. Three species, S. hirsutum, (Willd,:Fr.) Gray, S. rimosum Berk, and S. ochraceo-flavum (Schwein.) Ellis, have only ordinary hyphidia and belong to the subgenus Stereum. S. rimosum var africanum P.H.B. Talbot is reduced to synonymy with S. rimosum. S. vellereum Berkeley is reduced to synonymy with S. ochraceo-flavum. S. lobatum (Kunze:Fr.) Fr., S. australe Lloyd and S. sanguinolentum (Alb. & Schwein.:Fr.) Fr., possess pseudoacanthohyphidia and therefore belong to the subgenus Aculeatostereum. Representatives of the S. ostrea complex in South Africa are S. lobatum and S. australe, both considered well-defined species. S. durbanense van der Biji is considered a synonym of S. australe, S. illudens Berk, is the only species recognised in this study as possessing acanthohyphidia and therefore belongs to the subgenus Acanthastereum. A dichotomous key based on micro- and macromorphological characters is given for the seven South African species.
