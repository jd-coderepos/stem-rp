Bryopsis flanaganii Barton from the Natal coast appears to possess a monophasic life history in culture. Gametangia are formed from lateral branches and release anisogametes. Zygotes germinate immediately and develop into erect siphons similar to those of the parent thalli. These germlings do not, however, produce lateral branches under laboratory conditions. Nuclear divisions occurring during the formation of the gametes are mitotic and the chromosome number of B. flanaganii is 11. The chromosomes form an equatorial plate at metaphase and separate at anaphase leaving behind a persistent interzonal spindle. The nucleus of the zygote is characteristically large and persistent. S. Gametangia word op sytakke gevorm en stel anisogamete vry. Sigote ontkiem dadelik en ontwikkel tot regopstaande sifone soortgelyk aan. die van dié moedertalli. Hierdie kiemlinge vorm egter nie sytakke onder laboratorium-toestande nie. Kerndelings wat tot gameetvorming lei, is mitoties en die chromosoomgetal van B. flanaganii is 11. Die chromosome vorm ‘n ekwatoriale plaat tydens metafase, skei tydens anafase en vorm ‘n stabiele intersonale spoel. Die kern van die sigoot is kenmerkend groot en blywend. S.-Atr. Tydskr.
