The composition of alcoastal dune forest in the MIalazi Nature Reserve, Natal was studied three years after a fire and compared with adjacent unburnt forest. The two communities had similar plant densities but there was a marked decrease in biomass and diversity in the burnt community. Measured as density, basal area or canopy volume, Trema orientalis contributed over 70% of the regenerating burnt forest. Saplings of some forest trees were-present but those of Mimusops caffra, the dominant tree of the unburnt community, were absent from the burnt area. S. A fr. J.
