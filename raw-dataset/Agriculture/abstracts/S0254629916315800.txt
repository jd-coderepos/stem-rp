Raphionacme palustris Venter & Verhoeven, a new species from Natal, is described. It is recognized by the dichotomously branched stems, oblong-ovate leaves, mauve flowers and the long erect, very narrowly obovoid, normally solitary follicles. R. palustris belongs to Raphionacme Harv. sect. Raphionacme and is related to R. hirsuta (E. Mey.) R.A. Dyer. It is distinguished from R. hirsuta by its erect growth form, narrowly ovate corona lobes and the follicles as described above.
